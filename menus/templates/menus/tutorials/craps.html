How to Play Craps

Craps is a multiplayer game of chance in which players bet on the outcome of dice rolls. The game is split into two phases, the come-out phase and the point phase. Each phase is preceded by a round of betting. At the start of each game, one player is randomly selected to be the shooter, the person who rolls the dice. The other players are betting on the outcome of the shooter's dice rolls. In Craps, the shooter always rolls two dice.

Come-out Phase:

At the start of the come-out phase, each player is prompted to place bets on the Pass and Don't Pass lines. Players can bet any amount on one or both of these lines, but only one or the other line can win, so betting on both lines is not recommended. Once all players have placed their bets, the shooter can roll the dice.

If the shooter rolls a 7 or 11, this is known as a "natural", and the Pass bet line wins. If the shooter rolls a 2 or 3, this is known as "crapping out", and the Don't Pass bet line wins. If the shooter rolls a 12, this is also "crapping out", but neither bet line wins. If the shooter rolls a natural or craps out, the game ends immediately and payouts are distributed. If any other number is rolled, that number becomes the "point", and the game moves on to the point phase.

Point Phase:

At the start of the point phase, two different bet lines are available: Come and Don't Come. Once all players have placed their bets, the shooter rolls the dice again.

During the point phase, only two numbers on the dice can end the game. If the shooter rolls the point value again, then both the Pass and Come bet lines win and the game ends. If the shooter rolls a 7, the Don't Pass and Don't Come bet lines win and the game ends. If any other value is rolled, the shooter must roll the dice again. The game continues until the shooter either rolls the point or rolls a 7.


In Craps, all bet lines have a 1x payout. At the end of the game, players can ready up to play another round, where someone else will be chosen as the shooter.
