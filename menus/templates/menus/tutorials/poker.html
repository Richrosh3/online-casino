How to Play Poker

Online Casino features the most popular variety of Poker, Texas Hold 'Em. The goal is to form the best possible hand of five cards using your two "hole cards" (cards that belong only to you) and the five community cards, which are dealt out in three stages. All bets are placed into the "pot", and whoever has the best hand in the end takes all the money in the pot.

Poker is a turn-based game. On each player's turn, they can place a bet (also known as raising), check/call, or fold. Folding means you forfeit, losing whatever amount you may have already bet. Before any bets have been placed, both the pot and the "Price to call" are set at 0. The first bet that is placed will set the price to call, and it can be raised by subsequent players. All players are required to match the price to call, raise it, or fold.

After the initial bets, the dealer will deal the "flop", in which three community cards are revealed. A new round of betting begins after that, following the same rules as before. After that, the fourth community card is dealt (the "turn"), which is followed again by another round of betting, after which the final community card is dealt (the "river"). After the last community card is revealed, there is a final round of betting before hands are evaluated and payouts are made. The table below details the different types of hands, ordered from worst to best. The player with the best hand is the winner.
