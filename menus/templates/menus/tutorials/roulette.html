How to Play Roulette

Roulette is a single-player game of chance based around a ball and a spinning wheel. The wheel has 38 different pockets, numbered from 0 to 36 (there are two pockets for zero, 0 and 00). Aside from 0 and 00, which are colored green, each pocket is colored either red or black. For each round of play, the wheel is spun in one direction, and the ball is spun in the opposite direction. As momentum is lost, the ball will end up landing in one of the 38 different slots.

Players can place a wide variety of different bets on where the ball will land. All bets fall into one of two categories: inside and outside. Outside bets have higher odds but lower payouts. Below is an image of the betting table and a description of each kind of bet


Inside Bet Type Description Payout
Straight Bet on a single number. 35x
Split Bet on two horizontally or vertically adjacent numbers. 17x
Street Bet on one column of three numbers. 11x
Corner Bet on four numbers that meet at a corner in the betting table. 8x
Double Street Bet on two consecutive columns, totalling six consecutive numbers. 5x
Trio Bet on either 00-2-3 or 0-1-2 11x
Basket Bet on 0-00-1-2-3 6x
Outside Bet Type Description Payout
Low Bet on 1 to 18. 1x
High Bet on 19 to 36. 1x
Red Bet on all red pockets. 1x
Black Bet on all black pockets. 1x
Even Bet on all even numbers (0 and 00 do not count). 1x
Odd Bet on all odd numbers. 1x
Dozen Bet on one of three ranges of 12 numbers (1-12, 13-24, or 25-36). 2x
Column Bet on one of three columns of 12 numbers (see the "2:1" boxes on the betting table). 2x
Snake Bet on all the following: 1, 5, 9, 12, 14, 16, 19, 23, 27, 30, 32, 34 (forms a zigzag or snake-like pattern on the betting table). 2x
